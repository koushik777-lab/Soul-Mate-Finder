**Dating 105**: Can I hold your hand?

First dates can be exciting but tricky, especially regarding physical contact. Here's a guide to help:


1. Respect Personal Space: Start with a handshake or friendly hug, based on cultural norms and comfort levels.
2. Build Trust: Focus on conversation and shared interests, allowing physical contact to develop naturally.
3. Prioritize Consent: Always ask for permission before initiating any touch.
4. Read Signals: Pay attention to body language and verbal cues to gauge comfort.
5. Consider Context: Adapt physical contact based on the setting, whether public or private.
6. Communicate Openly: Discuss expectations and comfort levels to avoid misunderstandings.
7. Respect Boundaries: Honor your date's comfort level, even if it differs from yours.

By prioritizing respect, communication, and consent, you can ensure a positive and respectful first date experience.
